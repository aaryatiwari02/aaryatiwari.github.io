HevoData – Root Cause Analysis

Diagnosing why Free Trial → Subscription conversions stayed flat despite rising sign‑ups (Jan 2021 – Oct 2021).

Funnel Analysis Problem

Despite continuous month‑on‑month growth in sign‑ups, conversions from Free Trial → Subscription did not rise proportionally during Jan ’21 – Oct ’21. The objective is to identify root causes across external market dynamics and internal product experience.

Framework & Assumptions

  • The metric definition is unchanged throughout the analysis period.
  • The metric calculator & event logging are functional—sign‑ups and conversions are reliably recorded.
  • Diagnosis considers both External and Internal factors before proposing tests or fixes.

External Factors

  • Regulatory context: No clear regulation blocking trials → subs (given some conversions occur), but verify.
  • PR / Awareness: Sign‑ups are growing; confirm if this meets or lags expected growth targets and quality of traffic.
  • Price‑driven competition: Alternatives may offer better cost‑to‑benefit or incentives (bundles, more features in subscription, org‑wide seats).
  • Feature‑driven competition: Lower‑friction or more effective workflows could pull users away even at similar price points.
  • Indirect competition: Validate presence of simpler data‑pipelining tools; hypothesis: limited/no true no‑code substitutes.

Internal Factors

1) Onboarding as the Metric

Signup flow: landing → company email → name/password → profile setup (company questions).

  • Friction risk: Long, manual forms → potential drop‑offs; instrument step‑wise traction and error rates.

2) Pipelining as the Metric

  1. Source/Destination selection: Multiple selections allowed; no prompt clarifying free‑trial limitations or upsell options.
  2. Verification bypass: Users can proceed without email/phone verification (one‑time banner is easy to ignore). Risk of abuse via multiple fake trials; check reuse limits per email/phone.
  3. Usage metrics: Track active usage during trial—DAU, WAU—to distinguish one‑time tests vs. retained evaluators.
  4. Conversion CTAs: No prominent CTA/benefits on dashboard to upgrade trial → subscription.
  5. Trial start semantics: UI shows “trial not begun yet”; clarify exact trigger for trial start and ensure it’s enforceable.

Marketing & Strategy

  • Time window: Review campaigns run within Jan ’21 – Oct ’21; identify changes that altered sign‑up mix or intent.
  • Free‑trial value: Check if recent expansions in trial features increased top‑of‑funnel volume but diluted purchase intent.

Tech / Engineering

  • A/B or beta changes: Confirm any newly introduced or removed features in the period that could shift conversion.
  • UI changes: Ensure upgrade pathways (CTA/button placement) weren’t hidden or removed.

Tooling & Support

  • Friction / bugs: Trend support tickets, chatbot usage, and error logs to pinpoint confusing or failing steps.
