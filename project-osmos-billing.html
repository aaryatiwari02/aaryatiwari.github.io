Problem Statement

OSMOS's existing billing system was creating friction in the customer journey, leading to delayed payments, manual reconciliation efforts, and poor user experience that was impacting revenue growth.

System Limitations

  • Manual billing processes causing delays and errors
  • Limited payment gateway integrations
  • Poor visibility into billing status and history
  • Lack of automated invoice generation and tracking

Business Impact

  • Delayed revenue recognition affecting cash flow
  • High manual effort in finance team operations
  • Customer complaints about billing transparency
  • Difficulty in scaling billing operations

Retrospective

What Went Well

  • Strong VOC research led to user-centric feature development
  • Effective cross-functional collaboration and communication
  • Data-driven approach ensured feature prioritization alignment
  • Phased rollout minimized risks and enabled continuous improvement
  • Exceeded adoption and revenue targets significantly

Challenges & Learnings

  • Integration complexity with existing systems required additional planning
  • Payment gateway compliance requirements extended development timeline
  • User training and change management needed more focus initially
  • Balancing feature richness with system performance

Future Improvements

  • Earlier involvement of compliance and legal teams
  • More comprehensive user training and onboarding programs
  • Enhanced monitoring and analytics for system performance
  • Automated testing frameworks for billing accuracy
